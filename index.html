The week's story

This week the Church places before us a single sustained meditation: on hearing. It begins on Sunday with the Parable of the Sower and ends on Saturday with Our Lady, the woman who heard the Word better than anyone and bore Him into the world. In between, Isaiah opens his ear morning by morning, Jesus asks whether we hide the lamp He has given us, Jeremiah suffers for the Word he carries, and on Wednesday the great Solemnity of the Annunciation pauses everything.

The Holy Sacrifice of the Mass
Every word, gesture, colour and silence — explained from Scripture and Tradition

The Mass is the re-presentation of the one sacrifice of Calvary. When Jesus said "Do this in memory of me," the Greek word anamnesis does not mean merely recalling the past — it means making present. Every Mass is Calvary. Every altar is Golgotha.

The Church prays at every hour

When you pray the Divine Office, you are never praying alone. At this very moment, monks in monasteries, priests in their studies, and the faithful on buses and trains around the world are praying the same psalms, the same readings, the same prayers. The Liturgy of the Hours is the heartbeat of the Church's day.
